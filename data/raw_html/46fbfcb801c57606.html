    How Generative AI is transforming industries and building new startup opportunities

    How Generative AI is transforming industries and building new startup opportunities

    Synopsis

    India’s burgeoning AI adoption presents a unique opportunity for startups to innovate and thrive.

    It's no big secret that Generative AI (GenAI) is reshaping industries. Large corporations are already exploring and exploiting opportunities, using it to improve efficiencies, reduce costs, and grow their profit margins. However, the biggest beneficiaries of the GenAI revolution are likely to be startups.

    India has a booming digital economy with a rapidly growing entrepreneurial ecosystem that's embracing AI with open arms. Startup founders understand that GenAI represents not just a technology shift but a once-in-a-generation opportunity last seen only during the heydays of the internet revolution.

    For the uninitiated, GenAI is a type of artificial intelligence that creates new content, be it text, images, videos, music, or even computer code, by learning from existing data that it has been trained on. Unlike traditional AI, which only analyzes or classifies data, GenAI can produce new data based on the patterns it has identified and internalized.


    How Is Generative AI Transforming Industries In India?
    While AI is influencing almost every sector, some industries are experiencing faster and large-scale transformation. Here's an analysis of five industries where AI is fundamentally altering business models and creating significant startup opportunities.

    Live Events

        1) Information Technology (IT) and Software Services

        India’s IT industry is valued at $254 billion. A survey by EY India predicts that Generative AI could boost IT productivity by up to 45% over the next five years.

        This growth is coming from AI automating coding and software development, which significantly reduces development time. It's also coming from AI-driven customer support and chatbots that reduce human intervention in service delivery and AI-powered analytics that enable real-time decision-making and security monitoring across industries.

        Infosys recently raised its sales guidance for a third time, reporting a 10% increase in quarterly profits as its large clients were increasingly adopting its AI services. The market to provide similar services for SMEs is mostly untapped.

        2) Healthcare

        India faces a significant gap between the demand and supply of healthcare professionals. While the WHO recommends a government doctor to patient ratio of 1:1,000, India's current ratio is 1:10,189 people. There's a need for an additional 7.4 million healthcare workers by 2030.

        AI is already bridging this gap between doctors and patients. AI tools can detect diseases earlier using medical image analysis, while AI chatbots and virtual doctors are helping patients get instant medical advice and automate appointment booking along with records and prescription management. GenAI models are also speeding up drug discovery and clinical research.

        The National Health Authority and IIT Kanpur signed an MoU under Ayushman Bharat to develop digital public goods for AI research, benchmarking, and validating AI models. This is a sector enabler for startups building healthcare applications.

        3) Finance & Banking

        The Economic Survey 2025 notes that India's $2 trillion banking sector is aggressively integrating AI across their operations, facilitated by the India Stack.

        With hundreds of millions of transactions happening daily, AI can help prevent fraud by analyzing and identifying suspicious transactions in real-time. AI is also enabling hyper-personalized and faster banking experiences, offering investment advice previously provided by humans and automating loan approvals.

        A Mumbai-based conversational AI startup has implemented conversational bots that enabled the acquisition of over 50,000 new customers. The opportunity to build finance apps that help individuals and businesses automate savings, investments, and tax planning is enormous.

        4) Media & Entertainment

        India's content creation market is expected to increase from approximately $30 billion in 2024 to $480 billion by 2035. Approximately 92% of content creators in India are currently leveraging some form of artificial intelligence (AI) to streamline their production processes.

        AI creates personalized news summaries, videos, and blog articles in seconds and is increasingly being used for film dubbing and animation. AI-generated music and voiceovers are also reducing production costs for content creators.

        An Indian startup already offers a variety of audio content, including podcasts, audiobooks, and stories that use AI to convert scripts into audio series quickly, enabling creators to produce up to 10 times more content daily.

        5) E-Commerce and Customer Service

        India’s e-commerce market is expected to reach over $300 billion by 2030, and AI-driven chatbots and personalization tools are helping improve customer experiences.

        AI is already automating customer interactions and driving personalized product recommendations, but AI-powered chatbots are soon expected to handle customer queries 24/7 without any human agents.

        Both Flipkart and Amazon India have invested heavily in AI-powered solutions to improve customer retention. The demand for chatbot platforms for small and medium-sized businesses to automate customer service is largely untapped.

        Why Indian Startups Must Act Now

        Almost every other industry, like education, legal services, and logistics, has significant opportunities for startups building AI-powered solutions.

        GenAI is a revolution, and India is at a unique inflection point, where AI adoption is rapidly increasing. Risks with evolving regulatory and ethical frameworks do exist, but there are also investors actively looking to support those offering scalable AI solutions.

        If you have a problem-solving mindset and an AI-driven vision, this is your time to build the next big AI-powered startup in India.

        The author is an entrepreneur and board member of Harvard Alum
